# TMT Draft Guide — Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles

*Last updated: March 4, 2026*

---

## Format Overview

Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles (TMT) is a **small set with 190 cards** — significantly fewer than a standard set like Lorwyn Eclipsed (273 cards). This results in only **five supported enemy-color archetypes**, making the format quicker to learn but potentially less replayable long-term. The format supports both regular 8-player draft and Pick-Two draft.

The format is **slower than you might expect**. Only Red-White Alliance plays as true aggro. The other four archetypes are midrange or tempo strategies that build incremental advantage. Blue and Green have the strongest commons overall, making UR Artifacts and GU Mutagen naturally well-positioned.

**Key format rules to remember:**

- Legendary commons are frequent in TMT — never run a second copy of one
- Mutagen tokens are the format's glue mechanic — they are artifacts (for UR), trigger Disappear (for BG), and provide +1/+1 counters (for GU)
- Food tokens serve a similar multi-role purpose in BG
- There are common dual lands for all five enemy pairs, so mana is solid
- A 5-color good stuff pile is a viable fringe strategy with enough fixing

## Mechanics

### Sneak (White-Black)

![Foot Ninjas](https://cards.scryfall.io/normal/front/0/5/057a3ce5-39ff-4c04-b7fc-e06fc871a5bb.jpg?1771342230)

Sneak is a "fixed Ninjutsu." You cast a card for its Sneak cost during the **declare blockers step** on your turn by returning an **unblocked attacker** to your hand. If the Sneaked card is a creature, it enters tapped and attacking. Unlike Ninjutsu, Sneak works on noncreature spells too (e.g., Karai's Technique). The timing restriction (only declare blockers step) prevents some old Ninjutsu tricks but keeps the mechanic clean.

**Draft tip:** Value cheap evasive enablers (Dream Beavers, Featherbrained Filcher, April O'Neil) very highly — there are more Sneak payoffs than enablers.

### Mutagen Tokens (Green-Blue)

![Mutagen Token](https://cards.scryfall.io/normal/front/6/5/6559c423-449c-4e8e-8384-3ce78183e317.jpg?1771342158)

Mutagen is a colorless noncreature artifact token. You pay 1 mana, tap, and sacrifice it to put a +1/+1 counter on a creature (sorcery speed only). Individually modest, but Mutagen tokens are the format's Swiss Army knife — they count as artifacts for UR, trigger Disappear for BG, and directly feed the GU counters strategy.

**Draft tip:** Never cut Mutagen creators from your deck even if you're not in GU — they have cross-archetype value.

### Disappear (Black-Green)

![Putrid Pals](https://cards.scryfall.io/normal/front/3/a/3ad12a56-efae-4ef4-a54c-f78a0e763a82.jpg?1771342158)

Disappear is essentially Revolt from Aether Revolt — your cards get bonuses if a permanent left the battlefield under your control this turn. Enablers include sacrificing Food/Mutagen tokens, trading in combat, Sneak bouncing a creature, and flickering with Go Ninja Go.

**Draft tip:** Food tokens are the most reliable Disappear enablers since sacrificing them doesn't cost you board presence.

### Alliance (Red-White)

![EPF Point Squad](https://cards.scryfall.io/normal/front/0/0/00ece0a2-f981-4309-8b6f-f6a1fb77a00e.jpg?1771342158)

Alliance triggers whenever another creature enters the battlefield under your control. It rewards creature density and going wide. Tokens and blink effects (Go Ninja Go, The Neutrinos) trigger it efficiently.

**Draft tip:** Prioritize creatures that make additional creatures (Mechanized Ninja Cavalry, Jennika) to double-trigger Alliance.

### Artifacts (Blue-Red)

![Mouser Mark III](https://cards.scryfall.io/normal/front/2/4/243e392b-48b0-4e90-ae22-9a696f45e878.jpg?1771502635)

Not a named keyword mechanic, but a strong theme across blue and red. Many cards reward you for controlling, creating, or sacrificing artifacts. The payoffs range from card draw (Donatello, Turtle Techie) to massive combat buffs (Baxter Stockman) to removal (Brilliance Unleashed).

**Draft tip:** Food and Mutagen tokens both count as artifacts — don't underestimate incidental artifact generation.

## Color Power Rankings

| Rank | Color | Strength |
|------|-------|----------|
| 1    | Blue  | Best commons (Buzz Bots, Return to the Sewers, Donatello Turtle Techie). Deep artifact synergies. |
| 2    | Green | High quality commons (Primordial Pachyderm, Tenderize, Frog Butler). Great fixing. |
| 3    | Black | Premium removal (Anchovy & Banana Pizza, Stomped by the Foot). Strong in both supported pairs. |
| 4    | Red   | Good removal (Manhole Missile, Bot Bashing Time). Mouser Foundry is great artifact generation. |
| 5    | White | Weakest commons but strongest rares. Best evasive creatures for enabling Sneak. |

## Archetype Tier List

### Tier 1: UR Artifacts — "Machines"

![Baxter Stockman](https://cards.scryfall.io/normal/front/1/1/117b1341-2cf0-466e-b3a8-7e1afa42cd4c.jpg?1771342158)
![Brilliance Unleashed](https://cards.scryfall.io/normal/front/b/7/b7ab2110-5aad-46c9-8dc4-1eac24b6f46b.jpg?1771342158)

**Grade: A** | Signposts: Baxter Stockman, Brilliance Unleashed

The best deck in the format. UR Artifacts is a grindy midrange deck that snowballs through artifact synergies. The game plan is to play defensively in the early turns while building up artifacts, then slam expensive payoffs that dominate the board.

**How it wins:** Baxter Stockman gives an artifact creature +3/+0, first strike, and vigilance each turn. Donatello variants add permanent +1/+1 counters to artifacts. Together they turn any random Mouser token into a game-ending threat. Technodrome becomes a 12/9 trampler with first strike once fully buffed.

**Key cards:** Baxter Stockman, Brilliance Unleashed, Donatello (all versions), Krang Master Mind, Does Machines, Technodrome, Don & Raph Hard Science, Mouser Mark III, Mouser Foundry, Buzz Bots, Manhole Missile, Rock Soldiers, General Traag

**Draft strategy:** Take Donatello variants and Baxter Stockman over everything. Prioritize artifact creatures and artifact generators. Mouser Foundry is a premium common. Return to the Sewers is great — bounce + Mutagen token. Don't be afraid to play slow; your late game is dominant.

### Tier 1.5: BG Disappear — "Food & Sacrifice"

![Pizza Face, Gastromancer](https://cards.scryfall.io/normal/front/b/0/b03cf0bb-3207-4e8e-bb3f-e3e4367aa86e.jpg?1771342158)
![Tainted Treats](https://cards.scryfall.io/normal/front/f/a/fa684608-ecd3-43e2-99a1-71318f133e29.jpg?1771342158)

**Grade: A-** | Signposts: Pizza Face Gastromancer, Tainted Treats

Strong midrange deck that sacrifices Food and other tokens to trigger Disappear payoffs. The Food subtheme gives this deck excellent grinding power and a built-in life gain buffer.

**How it wins:** Pizza Face distributes +1/+1 counters every time Disappear triggers. Lord Dregg creates an army of flying Insects. West Wind Avatar is a huge body that draws cards. Putrid Pals becomes a 5/5 for 4 mana with Disappear active.

**Key cards:** Pizza Face, Tainted Treats, Lord Dregg, West Wind Avatar, Putrid Pals, Ice Cream Kitty, Foot Mystic, Anchovy & Banana Pizza, Stomped by the Foot, Ragamuffin Raptor, Courier of Comestibles, Frog Butler, Primordial Pachyderm

**Draft strategy:** Removal is your priority — Anchovy & Banana Pizza and Stomped by the Foot are the best black commons. Then pick up Disappear payoffs and Food generators. Frog Butler is premium (ramp + fixing + deathtouch). This deck can splash easily — consider pivoting to 5-color if you see Everything Pizza and fixing.

### Tier 2: WB Sneak — "Ninjas"

![Karai, Future of the Foot](https://cards.scryfall.io/normal/front/0/d/0dded2d4-1640-4431-809e-403b51b27db6.jpg?1771342158)
![Karai's Technique](https://cards.scryfall.io/normal/front/0/3/037fe188-3355-4724-b2eb-e2448fc55607.jpg?1771342158)

**Grade: B** | Signposts: Karai Future of the Foot, Karai's Technique

Tempo deck that uses cheap evasive creatures to enable Sneak effects. The best Sneak payoffs are powerful cards even cast at full cost, which gives this deck good floor consistency.

**How it wins:** Attack with cheap flyers (Dream Beavers, Featherbrained Filcher), then Sneak in threats mid-combat. Karai reanimates a creature when she deals combat damage. Leonardo, Leader in Blue warps blocking decisions. Karai's Technique is devastating removal mid-combat.

**Key cards:** Karai (both versions), Leonardo Leader in Blue, Leonardo Big Brother, Dream Beavers, Featherbrained Filcher, Splinter Hamato Yoshi, Foot Ninjas, Uneasy Alliance, Stomped by the Foot, Dimensional Exile, April O'Neil

**Draft strategy:** Take the best Sneak payoffs first (they're good standalone). Then prioritize 1-mana evasive enablers — these are less plentiful than payoffs. April O'Neil (Scry 2, can't be blocked by bigger creatures) is an excellent uncommon enabler.

### Tier 2: GU Mutagen — "+1/+1 Counters"

![Genghis Frog](https://cards.scryfall.io/normal/front/7/d/7df26085-eedb-4bdd-a60a-aabfbe9c3157.jpg?1771342158)
![Lessons from Life](https://cards.scryfall.io/normal/front/0/f/0f886117-aff3-4db7-9cf5-7cbe94c8cd02.jpg?1771342158)

**Grade: B+** | Signposts: Genghis Frog, Lessons from Life

Midrange counters deck with the highest raw card quality of any archetype, even if its synergies are shallow. Blue and green simply have the best commons, and Mutagen tokens provide steady incremental advantage.

**How it wins:** Grow creatures with Mutagen tokens and +1/+1 counter effects, then attack with evasive or protected threats like Punk Frogs. Michelangelo, Mutant BFF snowballs quickly. Lessons from Life provides ramp, counters, and card draw all in one card.

**Key cards:** Genghis Frog, Lessons from Life, Michelangelo Weirdness to 11, Michelangelo Mutant BFF, Ray Fillet, Slithering Cryptid, Punk Frogs, Primordial Pachyderm, Buzz Bots, Return to the Sewers, Frog Butler, Tenderize, Bespoke Bo

**Draft strategy:** Don't force Mutant tribal — Genghis Frog is the only card that rewards it. Instead, draft the best blue and green cards and let Mutagen tokens provide incidental synergy. This is an excellent base for 5-color splashing thanks to Frog Butler and Lessons from Life.

### Tier 2.5: RW Alliance — "Go Wide Aggro"

![The Neutrinos](https://cards.scryfall.io/normal/front/1/3/1308dadc-08a9-40bd-98a4-fb66d792e27d.jpg?1771342158)
![Go Ninja Go](https://cards.scryfall.io/normal/front/9/0/90e68cd8-ea76-4bd1-9199-474da9c3e8bf.jpg?1771342158)

**Grade: B-** | Signposts: The Neutrinos, Go Ninja Go

The format's only true aggro deck. Flood the board with cheap creatures, trigger Alliance repeatedly, and close games before midrange decks stabilize.

**How it wins:** The Neutrinos and Go Ninja Go blink creatures to re-trigger Alliance and ETB effects (unusual for Boros!). Mechanized Ninja Cavalry makes two bodies and two artifacts in one card. Raphael Tough Turtle pings the opponent every time a creature enters. Lita and Mighty Mutanimals reward creature density.

**Key cards:** The Neutrinos, Go Ninja Go, Lita, Raphael Tough Turtle, Mighty Mutanimals, Wingnut, Mechanized Ninja Cavalry, Jennika, Manhole Missile, Uneasy Alliance, Spicy Oatmeal Pizza, East Wind Avatar

**Draft strategy:** Curve is everything — prioritize 2-drops aggressively. Only include the very best 4+ mana cards. EPF Point Squad is a trap (clunky). Wingnut and Lita are much better Alliance payoffs. If the aggro plan doesn't come together, this deck struggles.

## Fringe Strategy: 5-Color Good Stuff

![Everything Pizza](https://cards.scryfall.io/normal/front/6/a/6a509a67-42c7-4bdc-a637-a1520d53ac33.jpg?1771342158)

**Viable but risky.** Pick up fixing aggressively — Everything Pizza (early fixing, late-game bomb), Frog Butler (ramp + deathtouch), dual lands, Escape Tunnel, and Omni-Cheese Pizza. Then play the best rares and removal from every color. Ragamuffin Raptor can recur Everything Pizza from the graveyard for a second use.

**The Last Ronin is worth splashing for in any deck** — it's widely considered the best card in the set.

## Top Commons by Color

### White
1. **Uneasy Alliance** — Pacifism with upside. Premium removal at common.
2. **April O'Neil, Kunoichi Trainee** — Scry 2 ETB, can't be blocked by larger creatures. Great Sneak enabler.
3. **Leonardo, Big Brother** — Solid body, excellent when Sneaked in for discount.

### Blue
1. **Buzz Bots** — One of the best commons in the set. Evasive, replaces itself on death, is an artifact.
2. **Return to the Sewers** — Bounce + Mutagen token. Tempo play that feeds multiple synergies.
3. **Donatello, Turtle Techie** — Artifact creature that draws a card on ETB with artifacts in play.

### Black
1. **Anchovy & Banana Pizza** — 4-mana removal that creates a Food. Workhorse card.
2. **Stomped by the Foot** — Cheap instant removal, upgradeable by sacrificing an artifact.
3. **Foot Mystic** — 2/4 lifelink that creates Food tokens with Disappear active.

### Red
1. **Manhole Missile** — 3 damage instant + card filtering. Excellent at all stages of the game.
2. **Mouser Foundry** — Creates two artifact tokens, later becomes removal. Synergy engine.
3. **Bot Bashing Time** — 4-mana sorcery that kills and exiles almost anything.

### Green
1. **Primordial Pachyderm** — 4/4 reach trample + life gain for 4 mana. Elite common.
2. **Tenderize** — Green's classic fight spell. Pairs well with deathtouchers.
3. **Frog Butler** — Ramp, fixing, deathtouch, and reach for 2 mana. Absurd utility.
